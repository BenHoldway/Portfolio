About Me

Hi, I'm Ben Holdway, a current English Games Design and Programming student at the University of Staffordshire wanting to specialise in Gameplay Programming.

I've been programming for nearly a decade now, working with languages from HTML to Python to C++ and C#. I've been a part of many different projects inside and out of games, both solo and in groups. Not all projects are shown, as they are not relevant, however a list of my experiences is listed below.

As a kid, growing up playing games like Minecraft and the Halo franchise, I was always questioning how these different mechanics and features were created. I spent hours trying to solve problems as I played, and wondered how they created such things. While I was beginning my GCSEs, something clicked and I realised that I could work on projects like this myself, without a big company team behind me. This is where I started my story towards games programming.

Hobbies and Interests

Within the gaming world, I still play a lot of games and watch content of these games as well. I enjoy a wide range of games, from sandbox games like Minecraft and Sea of Thieves to games like Subnautica and Phasmophobia, games that come under my favourite genre; horror.


As well as a programming enthusiast, I also enjoy spending my time in other creative ways. For some years now, I have put a lot of time into digital art. I have produced pieces for both myself and others, with being in online communities with other artists and making connections within those spaces.

I spend a lot of my time playing Dungeons and Dragons, a tabletop roleplaying game where you play as fantasy characters in stories and adventures. I have made countless different characters, all with lengthy backstories and interesting personalities. I try and indulge myself in a large range of character types to keep sessions interesting for myself.
